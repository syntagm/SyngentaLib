Syngenta | South Africa

The activities of Syngenta in South Africa span across crop protection and sunflower and vegetable seeds.

In all areas Syngenta invests in research and field development, manufacturing and supply, sales and marketing. Syngenta is a leading player in the South African market with a dedicated, highly trained distribution network to ensure quality service to our customers. These distributors are serviced by Regional Sales Managers, Key Account Managers and Sales Managers who provide technical as well as commercial support. Syngenta's key focus is to provide intergrated crop solutions, for both seeds and crop protection, developed to provide additional value to the growers and the rest of the food chain. Through our innovative solutions and top quality service, Syngenta invests for the successful future of South African sustainable agriculture ensuring abundant, safe and quality food.

Helping growers around the world

There will be 9 billion people on earth by 2050 and farmland is limited. By 2050, 4 billion of these people will be living in countries with water scarcities.

We are helping growers around the world to meet the challenges of the future i.e. grow more from less.

Syngenta plays a critical role in harnessing the power of plants to meet these challenges.

We are developing seeds that require less water and products that will allow crops to grow in dry conditions.

We are developing new higher yielding seeds and better ways to protect crops from insects, weeds and diseases.

What do different crop protection products do?

Herbicides remove weeds which compete for a farmer's crops for important resources namely: nutrients, water and light.

Insecticides control insect pests like whiteflies, aphids,mealybugs, thrips etc. Not only do insecticides keep crops healthy, they also are used to combat against disease spreading insects like ticks.

Fungicides protect plants from fungal diseases like powdery mildew, leaf rust, scab, citrus black spot, downy mildew etc.

Seed Treatment refers to the process whereby a fungicide, insecticide and/or nematode treatment is applied individually or in combination onto seeds before planting. Once planted seeds can become vulnerable to seed- and ground bearing diseases, early seasonal insects and nematodes if they have not undergone any form of seed treatment. Seed Treatments protect the seed and seedling during this critical stage of development.

Safety Data Sheet (SDS) What is it?

Safety Data Sheet (SDS) is a document that contains information to allow a hazardous material to be handled without causing harm to people, the environment or property. This term is widely used in Europe and in the Globally Harmonized System for Classification and Labelling of Chemicals (GHS) A Safety Data Sheet is the primary communication tool for HSE information between Syngenta and its customers. Many countries and regions require them when a material is supplied from Syngenta to a third party. Where there is no legal requirement to produce safety data sheets, Syngenta policy still requires that they be produced. Safety data sheets have a secondary use as an internal communication tool. They are also produced for some materials that are not transferred to third parties as a method for communicating information to manufacturing sites.
